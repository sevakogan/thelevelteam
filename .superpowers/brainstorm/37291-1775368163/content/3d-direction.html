Which 3D design direction feels right for TheLevelTeam?

This sets the tone for the entire landing page — the hero, transitions, backgrounds, and interactions

TLT

A — Floating Geometry

Vibe: Abstract, minimal, premium. Think Apple Vision Pro meets Stripe.

Floating 3D shapes (spheres, tori, cubes) drift through space. Soft ambient lighting. Glass materials with refraction. Mouse parallax shifts the camera slightly. Sections transition with smooth depth changes.

Tech: React Three Fiber + Drei. Lightweight meshes, PBR materials, post-processing bloom.

TLT

B — Particle Universe

Vibe: Deep space, data-driven, cinematic. Think Linear meets Vercel's globe.

Thousands of 3D particles form constellations, text, and shapes. Particles react to scroll and mouse movement. Connected by glowing lines that form networks. Sections emerge from the particle field. Stars become data points become UI.

Tech: React Three Fiber + custom shaders. GPU instanced particles (10K+), GPGPU for physics.

TLT

C — Living Terrain

Vibe: Organic, immersive, world-building. Think Webflow meets award-winning Awwwards sites.

A 3D landscape that morphs and evolves as you scroll. Mountains rise, oceans form, cities emerge. The terrain reflects the agency's journey with each section. Interactive globe for the portfolio. Volumetric fog and atmospheric lighting create depth.

Tech: React Three Fiber + custom terrain shaders. Displacement maps, fog, environment maps.

My recommendation: Option A (Floating Geometry) — it's the most versatile, performs best on mobile, and pairs perfectly with your existing glass/dark theme. The premium minimalism matches the agency brand without overwhelming the content. Options B and C are higher-risk: B needs GPU compute for particle physics, C needs complex terrain shaders that are hard to make look polished.
